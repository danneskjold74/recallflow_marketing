Who RevisitIQ is built for in salons and barbershops

Any shop where clients come back on a regular cycle and quietly drift when follow-up is inconsistent.

Hair salons

Cuts, color, and highlights run on 4–8 week cycles. Clients who loved the work often mean to rebook and simply forget.

Barbershops

Trims run every 2–4 weeks. A regular who goes six weeks is already drifting toward whoever is top of mind.

Beauty, skin & waxing

Facials, lash fills, brow shaping, and wax services run on predictable cycles. Follow-up decides whether lapsed clients come back to you.

Nail salons

Manicures run 2–4 weeks and fills every 2–3. Clients who quietly stopped rarely speak up — they just book somewhere else.

What a recall text looks like

Short, specific, and built to bring one overdue client back at a time — not a promotional blast.

Hair salon

Hi Sarah, it's been about 7 weeks since your last cut at Lumen Salon. Want to book your next visit? Reply BOOK or call (555) 123-4567. Reply STOP to opt out.

Barbershop

Hey Marcus — it's been 5 weeks since your last trim at Broadway Cuts. Ready for a touch-up? Reply BOOK or text us to schedule. Reply STOP to opt out.

You write your own message in RevisitIQ — these are examples of what a short, practical recall text usually looks like.

Common questions

Do I need to replace my salon booking software?

No. RevisitIQ works alongside your existing booking system. It handles the one thing most salon software doesn't — tracking overdue clients and sending recall messages.

Can I upload my client list from my current system?

Yes. Export a CSV from your salon software or spreadsheet and upload it to RevisitIQ. No manual re-entry needed.

What do the recall texts look like?

Short, practical messages — like a friendly reminder that it's time for their next visit. Not marketing blasts or promotional spam.

What if a client opts out?

Clients can opt out of recall messages at any time. RevisitIQ handles opt-outs automatically so you stay compliant.

Is this a CRM or marketing platform?

No. RevisitIQ is a lightweight recall overlay. It does one thing: helps you see which clients are overdue and follow up before they drift.
